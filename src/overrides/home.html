OpenCollaboration

An open-authored book for modern software engineering. The starting point for advancing our company's digital market competitiveness.

Our premise

Successful software companies adapt to rapid technological, economic, and political changes to viably compete in any market. This fact introduces the paradox of providing a stable and low-latency experience while continuously responding to user feedback and regulatory compliance.

Within OpenCollaboration, we discuss a vertical slice of strategies that have proven useful for a broad spectrum of organizations. The three sections of this book provide the foundation for building an autonomous, flexible, and collaborative environment at the core of our company.

The Anatomy of a Software Company

How do we encourage organic collaboration while limiting noise? Part I discusses strategies for cultivating a growth mindset and sustainably increasing the complexity of our product.

This section covers policies to advance the professional expertise of our employees while expanding the number of teams within our organization.

The Anatomy of a Code Change

Part III analyzes good practices for the day-to-day work of engineers to achieve software products with high adaptability and stability. We present workflows and automation concepts to increase the frequency of our software deliveries.

Our Roadmap

The only kind of writing is rewriting

OpenCollaboration is being rewritten in public and is currently released as a preview. Practicing the contents of this book, we delivery chapters and edits continously. The current version is v0.14.2.

Since the initial release of OpenCollaboration, we have published the chapters of Part I and III with various levels of refinement. Cooking the core subject matter into easily digestable works of writing with consistent flavour, tone, and style. After this substantial edit and rewrite we'll release the chapters of Part II sequentially.

The book is available open source with permissive licensing and is currently written and maintained by Daniel Lanner. We welcome expert community involvement in form of edits and chapters. Get in touch or open an issue on GitHub to discuss contributions or request additional chapters.
